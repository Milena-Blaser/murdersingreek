Hera

“You said you went to a mortal wedding?”, I ask. “Yes!”, Hera says very enthusiastically. “Did you know they do funny dances at their weddings sometimes? I was in shock when I saw the groom and the bride dance the way they did, you should see what weddings have become-” “You want to tell me their names and what you did later that day?”, I interrupt her enthusiastic rambling. “Sure!”, she says, a bit less motivated than before. “Their names are Tyler and Jess and later that day, I was avenging the victims of domestic abuse. Some things the people went through… I just couldn’t let it go”, Hera explained, tearing up. I quickly write it down and focus on Hera again. “A lot of the pain they have to live through is my fault”, she continues. “I just wanted to make it better. At least for some of them”, tears are rolling down her cheeks. “The way people are is not your fault”, I try to console Hera. “And everyone on Olympus knows, you just want what’s best for others. Mortal or not.” “I have all I need. Thank you, Hera.”
