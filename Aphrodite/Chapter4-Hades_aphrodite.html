Hades

“So you were in court? In the underworld?”, I ask Hades. “You know I don’t like it when you call it the underworld”, he mumbles. I raise an eyebrow and he sighs. “Yes, I was. There was a soul on trial for putting milk before cereal. It was an almost blasphemous case, almost as if just to spite me. But in the end it was a clear case for me”, Hades says. “So how long were you in court for?” “Pretty much all day”, he scratches his neck. I look him in the eyes. “If it was a clear case, why’d it take all day?” He smirks. “Well”, he starts. “Let’s say the mortal was very talkative and tried to talk his way out. Somehow he knew I wouldn’t interrupt him.” I chuckle a little. “Yes, it’s very hard to tell with you. You softie”, I say. “But that would be all. Thank you, Hades.”
